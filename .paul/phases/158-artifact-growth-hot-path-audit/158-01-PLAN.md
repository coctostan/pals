<objective>
## Goal
Produce an evidence-backed audit of how `.paul/` artifacts have grown, which artifact classes now dominate footprint, and which files are repeatedly pulled into Pi-era planning and lifecycle flows strongly enough to create recurring context cost.

## Purpose
This milestone is not trying to redesign PALS blind. It needs a factual baseline for the user's concern that long-lived artifacts such as `.paul/STATE.md` and `.paul/ROADMAP.md` may be bloating over time and getting re-read often enough to waste context. Phase 158 should separate artifact-size growth, archive accumulation, and repeat-read hot paths before Phase 159 audits workflow fluff and Phase 160 ranks final fixes.
