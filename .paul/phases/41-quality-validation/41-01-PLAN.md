<task type="auto">
  <name>Task 1: Research 12 frameworks' quality & validation mechanics</name>
  <files>.paul/phases/41-quality-validation/quality-research.md</files>
  <action>
    Research 12 frameworks using 4 parallel subagent batches (3 frameworks each).

    **Frameworks:** Aider, Superpowers, GPT Pilot, SWE-agent, OpenHands, Devin, OpenSpec, BMAD, MetaGPT, Cursor, Cline, Continue.dev

    **7 Research Dimensions (quality/validation focused):**
    1. **Quality gate timing** — When do quality checks run? Pre-commit, post-task, post-plan, CI? What triggers them?
    2. **Regression detection** — How are regressions caught? Baseline comparison, snapshot testing, diff-based, assertion count tracking?
    3. **Lint/format integration** — How are linters, formatters, type checkers integrated? Auto-fix behavior? Which categories block vs warn?
    4. **Quality metrics & trending** — What metrics are tracked over time? How are trends surfaced? Dashboard, CLI, reports?
    5. **Gate configurability** — Can users configure strictness? Per-category, per-project, per-phase? Override mechanisms?
    6. **Validation feedback** — How is quality feedback presented to the agent/user? Structured reports, inline annotations, summaries?
    7. **Recovery from quality failures** — What happens when quality gates fail? Auto-fix, retry, escalate, skip?

    **Subagent batch assignment:**
    - Batch 1: Aider, Superpowers, GPT Pilot
    - Batch 2: SWE-agent, OpenHands, Devin
    - Batch 3: OpenSpec, BMAD, MetaGPT
    - Batch 4: Cursor, Cline, Continue.dev

    Each subagent searches for public docs, repos, and blog posts about the framework's quality validation approach. Focus on what's unique or better than WALT's current approach.

    **Output format:** Same as Phases 38-40 — per-framework findings across all 7 dimensions, comparison matrix, candidate techniques list, adopt/adapt/reject decision table.

    **Also research non-framework approaches:**
    - SonarQube / SonarCloud quality gate model
    - GitHub Actions quality workflows
    - Pre-commit hook patterns
    - ESLint/Prettier CI integration patterns

    These inform "what WALT could learn from traditional quality tooling."
