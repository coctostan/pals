<objective>
## Goal
Make PALS itself bloat less as it creates and consumes `ROADMAP.md`, and define the process-level repair path for roadmap bloat once it inevitably appears.

## Purpose
The point of v2.43 is not merely to tidy this repository's `.paul/ROADMAP.md`; that file is evidence of the failure mode. PALS needs a durable active-window roadmap convention in its templates and workflows so future projects keep current routing cheap, push completed detail into authoritative archives, and recover when accumulated roadmap history grows too large.

## Output
Update PALS kernel/process artifacts so they specify:
- `ROADMAP.md` active-window shape and completed-history archive links.
- Milestone completion behavior that archives completed roadmap detail instead of re-embedding it inline forever.
- Roadmap management repair behavior for already-bloated roadmaps.
- Plan/read discipline that treats current milestone slices as hot and completed history as cold.

This plan uses the current repo's `.paul/ROADMAP.md` only as evidence of bloat; it does not structurally compact this project's live roadmap as the deliverable.
</objective>
