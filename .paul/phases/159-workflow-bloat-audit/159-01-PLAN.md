## Goal
Produce an evidence-backed audit of how Pi-facing workflow layers, skill wrappers, and shared workflow prose amplify recurring context cost around hot artifacts, especially `.paul/STATE.md` and `.paul/ROADMAP.md`.

Phase 158 already separated artifact growth from hot-path artifact recurrence. Phase 159 should now measure the workflow side of the same problem: repeated required-reading, duplicated lifecycle prose, broad read scopes, and Pi-guided prompt-shape patterns that may be making hot artifacts feel more expensive than the artifact files alone would justify. The output should give Phase 160 a clean workflow-side evidence set rather than another round of intuition.
